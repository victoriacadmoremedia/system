          Typography

          Typography defines how content is read, scanned and understood across Gen 2 Media Sites.

          Overview

          Typography defines how content is read, scanned and understood across Gen 2 Media Sites.

          The typography system prioritises readability, clear hierarchy and consistency to support long-form, academic and media-rich content.

          This page defines typographic roles, scales and usage principles.

          Client branding and site-specific adjustments are documented under White labelling.

          Principles

          Gen 2 typography is content-first and neutral by default.
          It exists to support reading and comprehension rather than visual expression.

          Hierarchy must be clear and predictable so users can understand page structure at a glance. Font sizes and spacing follow a defined scale to avoid arbitrary variation and to maintain rhythm across page types.

          Typographic roles

          Typography in Gen 2 is defined by role, not by individual font styles.
          Roles describe how text is used within the interface.

          Body text is used for primary reading content, including articles, descriptive copy and supporting text. It is optimised for long-form reading, with comfortable line length, spacing and a regular font weight.

          Headings establish structure and hierarchy. They support scanning and content organisation, and heading levels must reflect document structure without skipping levels.

          UI text supports interaction and navigation, such as buttons, links, navigation items, form labels and metadata. Labels should remain concise and prioritise clarity over tone.

          Metadata and supporting text provides context without distraction. It should remain visually subordinate to body text while maintaining sufficient contrast for accessibility.

          Scale, spacing and flow

          Gen 2 uses a defined type scale shared across page types to maintain consistent hierarchy and rhythm. Only documented scale steps should be used.

          Readable line length is critical for long-form content. Text columns should avoid excessive width and favour constrained reading areas. Body text uses generous line height to support scanning, while headings may use tighter spacing. Spacing remains consistent across similar roles.

          Text is left-aligned by default to support reading order. Centred text should be used sparingly, and justified text is avoided due to readability issues.

          Emphasis, links and accessibility

          Emphasis should be used sparingly and conveyed through font weight or semantic elements rather than colour alone. Excessive bolding within body content should be avoided.

          Links should be clearly identifiable without disrupting reading flow or becoming indistinguishable from surrounding text.

          Typography must support inclusive use. Text must be resizable without loss of content or functionality, contrast must remain sufficient, and spacing must not hinder readability.

          Typography behaves consistently across all contexts.
          Where typography appears on emphasis surfaces, contrast may be adjusted without altering hierarchy or meaning.

          Summary

          Typography in Gen 2 is role-driven, predictable and readability-focused.
          Themes may adapt visual appearance, but not typographic intent.

          Gen 2 typography prioritises clarity, hierarchy and readability across all contexts.
