          Spacing

          Spacing defines how rhythm, proximity and separation are applied across Gen 2 Media Sites.

          Overview

          The spacing system establishes consistent visual rhythm and clear relationships between elements to support readability, scanning and comprehension.

          This page defines spacing principles and usage guidance.

          Any site-specific adjustments are documented under White labelling.

          Principles

          Gen 2 spacing is content-first and structural by default.
          It exists to organise information clearly and support reading, scanning and interaction.

          Spacing reinforces hierarchy and grouping. Related elements should feel visually connected, while unrelated elements should be clearly separated.

          Spacing decisions are deliberate and predictable. Consistent use of spacing reduces visual noise and helps users understand structure without relying on additional styling.

          Spacing and hierarchy

          Spacing plays a key role in establishing hierarchy across the interface.

          Larger spacing values are used to separate major sections and regions, while smaller spacing values are used within components and content groups. This creates a clear visual rhythm that supports both scanning and focused reading.

          Spacing should work alongside typography and layout to communicate structure. It should not be used decoratively or arbitrarily.

          Rhythm and consistency

          Gen 2 uses a defined spacing scale to maintain consistent rhythm across page types and content densities.

          Only documented spacing steps should be used. Ad-hoc margins or padding introduce inconsistency and undermine the system.

          Spacing remains consistent across similar roles and patterns, even when content density or visual context changes.

          Proximity and grouping

          Spacing communicates relationships between elements.

          Elements that belong together should be visually grouped through proximity. Increased spacing should signal separation between distinct sections, components or content groups.

          Spacing should never be the sole indicator of meaning. Grouping and hierarchy must also be reinforced through structure, alignment and semantics.

          Responsive behaviour

          As layouts adapt across screen sizes, spacing may adjust to suit available space and reading comfort.

          While spacing values may scale or compress responsively, the relative relationships between elements must remain intact. Hierarchy and grouping should not change as spacing adapts.

          Accessibility and readability

          Spacing must support inclusive use.

          Adequate spacing improves readability, touch targeting and focus clarity. Content must remain readable when zoomed or resized, and spacing should not cause content overlap or loss of information.

          Consistent spacing also supports cognitive accessibility by reducing visual clutter and improving predictability.

          Summary

          Spacing in Gen 2 provides a consistent rhythm and clear visual structure for content and interaction.
          It reinforces hierarchy and relationships without drawing attention to itself.

          Gen 2 spacing uses consistent scales and predictable relationships to support rhythm, hierarchy and readability across all contexts.
