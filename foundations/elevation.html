          Elevation

          Elevation defines how depth and surface separation are communicated across Gen 2 Media Sites.

          Overview

          Elevation uses depth to distinguish surfaces, reinforce hierarchy and support focus within the interface.

          Client branding and site-specific adjustments are documented under White labelling.

          Principles

          Gen 2 elevation is functional and restrained by default. It exists to clarify structure and focus, not to add decoration.

          Elevation reinforces hierarchy by separating foreground surfaces from background content. Similar surfaces should appear at consistent depths across the system.

          Elevation should work alongside layout and spacing. It must not be used to compensate for unclear structure or visual clutter.

          Surfaces and hierarchy

          Elevation is applied to surfaces, not individual elements.

          Common elevated surfaces include cards, panels and overlays. These surfaces sit above base content to indicate importance, focus or temporary interaction.

          Lower elevation signals foundational content. Increased elevation signals foreground focus or interaction. Elevation should remain predictable and limited.

          Interaction and accessibility

          Elevation may respond subtly to interaction or state, such as hover or focus, reinforcing affordance without distraction.

          Depth cues should not rely on shadow alone. Borders, spacing and contrast should support separation where needed, and elevation must not interfere with readability, focus order or keyboard navigation.

          Summary

          Elevation in Gen 2 provides clear surface separation and focus through consistent, restrained use of depth.

          Gen 2 elevation uses restrained, consistent depth to reinforce hierarchy and surface separation across all contexts.
