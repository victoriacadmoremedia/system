          Layout

          Layout defines how content is structured and organised across Gen 2 Media Sites.

          Overview

          The layout system prioritises clarity, predictability and flexibility to support a wide range of content types, from long-form reading to media-rich and discovery-led pages.

          This page defines layout principles and common structural patterns.

          Detailed compositions and variations are documented under Page types and Patterns.

          Principles

          Gen 2 layout is content-first and functional by default.
          It exists to organise information clearly and support reading, scanning and interaction.

          Layout patterns are predictable and reusable. Users should be able to understand page structure quickly and apply that understanding across the system.

          Layout supports hierarchy without drawing attention to itself. Structural decisions should feel intentional but unobtrusive.

          Layout structure

          Layout in Gen 2 is defined by structural patterns rather than fixed page designs.

          These patterns describe how primary content, supporting content and navigation relate to one another. They are reused across page types and contexts to maintain consistency and reduce cognitive load.

          Regardless of structure, primary content should remain clearly identifiable and visually prioritised.

          Common layout structures

          Gen 2 uses a small set of recurring layout structures to organise content consistently across the system.

          These structures describe how content is arranged, not how individual pages are styled.

          Single-column reading

          A focused layout where content flows vertically within a constrained width, optimised for long-form reading. 

          <div class="l-page">
  <main class="l-layout l-layout--single" id="main-content">
    <article class="l-region l-reading">
      <header class="l-reading__header">
        <h1>Page title</h1>
        <p>One-sentence definition under the H1.</p>
      </header>

      <section class="l-reading__body">
        <p>Primary reading content…</p>
      </section>
    </article>
  </main>
</div>

          Main with supporting panel

          A layout where primary content is accompanied by secondary information such as metadata, filters or related content. 

          <div class="l-page">
  <main class="l-layout l-layout--main-panel" id="main-content">
    <section class="l-region l-main" aria-label="Main content">
      <h1>Primary content</h1>
      <p>Main reading area…</p>
    </section>

    <aside class="l-region l-panel" aria-label="Supporting content">
      <p>Supporting content…</p>
    </aside>
  </main>
</div>

          Content with navigation

          A layout where navigational structures sit alongside primary content to support browsing and categorisation. 

          <div class="l-page">
  <main class="l-layout l-layout--nav-content" id="main-content">
    <nav class="l-region l-nav" aria-label="Section navigation">
      <ul class="l-nav__list">
        <li class="l-nav__item"><a class="l-nav__link" href="#">Navigation item</a></li>
        <li class="l-nav__item"><a class="l-nav__link" href="#">Navigation item</a></li>
      </ul>
    </nav>

    <section class="l-region l-content" aria-label="Content">
      <article class="l-content__body">
        <h1>Content area</h1>
        <p>Primary content…</p>
      </article>
    </section>
  </main>
</div>

          Media-led layout

          A layout where media establishes the primary visual and structural focus of the page. 

          <div class="l-page">
  <main class="l-layout l-layout--media-led" id="main-content">
    <section class="l-region l-media" aria-label="Primary media">
      <figure class="l-media__figure">
        <div class="l-media__frame" role="region" aria-label="Media player"></div>
        <figcaption class="l-media__caption">Media context (optional)</figcaption>
      </figure>
    </section>

    <section class="l-region l-media-content" aria-label="Supporting content">
      <h1>Supporting content</h1>
      <p>Description, metadata, actions…</p>
    </section>
  </main>
</div>

          Grid-based content

          A layout that presents multiple items using a consistent grid structure, commonly used for browsing and discovery. 

          <div class="l-page">
  <main class="l-layout" id="main-content">
    <section class="l-region" aria-label="Results">
      <h1>Browse</h1>

      <ul class="l-grid" aria-label="Content list">
        <li class="l-grid__item">Item</li>
        <li class="l-grid__item">Item</li>
        <li class="l-grid__item">Item</li>
        <li class="l-grid__item">Item</li>
      </ul>
    </section>
  </main>
</div>

          Content regions and flow

          Layouts are composed of distinct regions, such as headers, main content areas, supporting panels and footers.

          Each region has a clear purpose within the page. Content density may vary between regions, but overall hierarchy and reading order must remain clear.

          As layouts adapt across screen sizes, regions may reflow or stack. Meaning, priority and reading flow should not change.

          Accessibility and consistency

          Layout must support inclusive use.

          Reading order should follow the visual structure of the page. Content must not rely on position alone to convey meaning, and layouts must remain usable when zoomed, resized or viewed with assistive technologies.

          Spacing and alignment should support readability and interaction without introducing unnecessary complexity.

          Summary

          Layout in Gen 2 provides a predictable structural foundation for content and interaction.
          Patterns prioritise clarity, hierarchy and reuse over visual novelty.

          Gen 2 layout defines consistent structural patterns that support content hierarchy and reading flow across page types and contextsstrong
